Biography

Security, Privacy, and Resilience in Future Communication Systems

I am a Ph.D. student in Computer Science at Utah State University. My research focuses on security, privacy, and resilience in mobile networks and systems, wearable healthcare technologies, and AI-enabled infrastructures. I am particularly interested in privacy-preserving architectures, cellular identifier protection, differential privacy, and usable security for real-world systems.

My recent work investigates privacy-exposing identifiers in 4G/5G networks, end-to-end security modeling for wearable systems, password breach risk communication, and sustainable machine learning practices.
